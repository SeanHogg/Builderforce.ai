# Product Requirements Document: Risk Identification (Top 3)

## Problem & Goal

**Problem:** Inadequate identification and prioritization of critical risks can lead to project delays, budget overruns, and failure to meet objectives.
**Goal:** To proactively identify and document the top three highest-priority risks (based on a combination of severity and likelihood) for the [Project Name] project, enabling focused mitigation efforts.

## Target Users / ICP Roles

*   Project Manager
*   Technical Lead
*   Key Stakeholders (e.g., Product Owner, Engineering Manager)

## Scope

This document defines the process and output for identifying the top three risks. It includes:

*   Defining the criteria for risk severity and likelihood.
*   Establishing a method for combining severity and likelihood to determine priority.
*   Documenting the top three identified risks with their rationale.

## Functional Requirements

1.  **Risk Identification:** The project team shall brainstorm potential risks across all relevant project dimensions (technical, resource, schedule, scope, external dependencies, etc.).
2.  **Risk Assessment (Severity):** Each identified risk shall be assessed for its potential impact on project objectives (e.g., on a scale of 1-5).
3.  **Risk Assessment (Likelihood):** Each identified risk shall be assessed for its probability of occurrence (e.g., on a scale of 1-5).
4.  **Risk Prioritization:** A risk score shall be calculated by multiplying severity and likelihood.
5.  **Top Risk Selection:** The three risks with the highest risk scores shall be identified and documented.

## Acceptance Criteria

*   A comprehensive list of potential project risks is generated.
*   Each risk is assigned a severity score (1-5).
*   Each risk is assigned a likelihood score (1-5).
*   A risk prioritization matrix or equivalent method is used to calculate risk scores.
*   The top three risks are clearly identified and documented.
*   Rationale for the severity and likelihood assessment of the top three risks is provided.

## Out of Scope

*   Detailed risk mitigation plans.
*   Risk monitoring and control processes.
*   Risk communication strategies beyond documenting the top three risks.
*   A complete risk register for all identified risks (only the top three are in scope for this task).
